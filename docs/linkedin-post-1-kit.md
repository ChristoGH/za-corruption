## 3. Chart menu — pick insights that are genuinely *new*

The bar you set: reveal something the daily reporting can't. Journalists summarize
*what was said*; only the parsed corpus can show *how the record behaves*. Generate
all of these in `corpus_stats.py`, then choose the 3 with the strongest signal:

1. **Word-share by procedural role** (almost certainly Insight A). Nobody has
   quantified how much of the Commission is questions vs answers. Safe: roles, not
   conduct.
2. **Turn-length tempo over time.** Mean/median answer length per day. If it visibly
   shifts at phase boundaries or for specific sittings, that's a "testimony has a
   heartbeat" story — novel and entirely structural.
3. **Interruption proxy:** rate of very short turns (< ~15 words) per day — a
   defensible proxy for contested, rapid exchanges. Label it honestly as a proxy.
4. **Sitting-pattern calendar heatmap:** hearing days on a calendar — recesses,
   intensity bursts. Instantly readable for non-technical viewers.
5. **Speaker-label inventory:** how many distinct voices appear ([N] labels) and the
   long tail — "the Commission is far bigger than the five names you know."
6. **Pages-per-day trend** with cumulative pages — the "this record is now [N]× the
   length of War and Peace" comparison is irresistible and harmless.

**Avoid in Post #1:** anything ranking *named individuals* by how often they're
mentioned. Mention-frequency leaderboards read as insinuation. Save entity-level
material for M3's network post, framed as co-occurrence, and keep it to people in
public roles already extensively reported.
