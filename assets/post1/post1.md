If you're a proud South African (like me), the Madlanga Commission has had your attention. In the Strydom household it has quietly wrecked our evening Netflix algorithm! To make more sense of it all I threw some technology at it: download every transcript and see what 106 hearing days of testimony actually add up to.  And of course to interrogate a vector store and build a graph db in the process, for good measure.
First finding: the record stands at 18,192 pages — about fifteen times the page count of Tolstoy's War and Peace (six and a half times the words). The commission cleared the original inside its first month and has only slowed for weekends and the Christmas season.  Is it ok to rename the findings Crime and Corruption?  And too early for Crime and Punishment...
Second finding: that number was wrong until this week. My pipeline's integrity checks caught two transcripts published twice in the official record — one filed under the wrong hearing day, one a partial re-upload whose filename claimed to be the full version. Nearly 300 phantom pages, gone. 
Also, it seems transcripts need to be approved before hitting public access.  That means my numbers are a few days old.  Not so cool.  But then the YouTube video for each day is available on demand - now there is work for another branch. 
If merely counting the pages takes this much care, imagine what reading them takes...
