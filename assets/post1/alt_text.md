# Chart alt text (for LinkedIn accessibility)

## cumulative_pages
Area chart: cumulative transcript pages over time reach 18,192 pages, about 15 times the length of War and Peace. A gold dotted line at 1,225 pages (one War and Peace) sits near the bottom, crossed within the first weeks; the December–January recess shows as a plateau.

## pages_per_day
Bar chart: transcript pages for each hearing day on a calendar axis; the longest day (462 pages) is highlighted, and the December recess shows as a gap. Several short sittings (rulings, postponements) appear as small bars.

## turn_tempo
Line chart: 5-day rolling median of words per speaker turn across hearing days with at least 50 turns, on a calendar axis. The rolling window is over hearing days in sequence, not calendar days; the line breaks across the December–January recess rather than bridging it.

## interruption_proxy
Line chart: 5-day rolling share of turns under 15 words per hearing day — a proxy for rapid, contested back-and-forth — on a calendar axis. The rolling window is over hearing days in sequence, not calendar days; the line breaks across the December–January recess rather than bridging it.

## role_word_share
Horizontal bar chart of share of words spoken: Counsel 61.3%, then Witnesses 29.2%, The bench 9.5%. Role attribution is heuristic and provisional.

## marathon_days
Horizontal bar chart of the 10 longest hearing days by transcript pages; the longest is Day 43 (462 pages). Days with no testifying witness are labelled 'legal argument'.
